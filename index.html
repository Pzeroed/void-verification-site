Void Constitution & Law Code

Version: v2026.01.13

Section 0 – Purpose

  • Protect members from harassment, abuse, and disruptive behavior.
  • Provide clear, enforceable rules with no loopholes.
  • Maintain a safe, teen-appropriate environment.
  • Ensure consistent, fair, and transparent enforcement by staff.
  • All members must read and understand this document. Ignorance of rules is not an excuse.

Section 1 – Member Rights

  • 1.01 Right to Respect – Every member must be treated politely and with dignity. Personal attacks, harassment, mocking, or belittling are strictly prohibited.
  • 1.02 Right to Voice – Members may participate in discussions, polls, and suggestion channels, provided communication is respectful.
  • 1.03 Right to Safety – Members must feel safe from threats, intimidation, or harassment. Any direct or implied actions that threaten safety violate this right.
  • 1.04 Right to Privacy – Members’ personal info (real names, addresses, schools, social media) cannot be shared without consent. Violations are severe.
  • 1.05 Right to Fair Enforcement – Members are entitled to consistent enforcement and may appeal actions they consider unfair. Staff must provide reasoning and cite rule numbers.
  • 1.06 Right to Participation – Members may propose rule changes and provide suggestions; staff must review them respectfully.
  • 1.07 Right to Accountability – Staff are accountable for fair enforcement. Members may request clarifications or explanations regarding decisions affecting them.
  • 1.08 Right to Education – Members may access guidance to understand rules, violations, and consequences.
  • 1.09 Right to Digital Safety – Members must be protected from malware, phishing, and malicious content.
  • 1.10 Right to Inclusion – No discrimination based on race, gender, sexual orientation, religion, disability, or other personal characteristics.
  • 1.11 Right to Consent – Members’ content cannot be shared, edited, or reposted without explicit permission.
  • 1.12 Right to Appeal – Members may appeal enforcement actions respectfully, citing relevant rule numbers.

Section 2 – Vocabulary / Definitions

  • 2.01 Constructive Criticism – Feedback intended to help improve behavior or content, delivered respectfully. Must not include personal attacks or harassment. Mislabeling rude behavior as “constructive criticism” is prohibited.
  • 2.02 Harassment – Repeated, targeted, or deliberate behavior intended to annoy, intimidate, or harm a member.
  • 2.03 Spam – Repeated messages, images, links, or emojis that disrupt conversation flow.
  • 2.04 Minor Offense – Small infractions interfering with chat, media, or voice flow (Class 1).
  • 2.05 Moderate Offense – More serious or repeated violations, including continued minor offenses or mild harassment (Class 2).
  • 2.06 Severe Offense – Dangerous or harmful actions, repeated harassment, or behavior threatening server integrity (Class 3).
  • 2.07 Spoiler – Information revealing plot, outcome, or surprises. Must be properly tagged.
  • 2.08 Role Abuse – Misusing roles or permissions to bypass rules or disrupt server operation.
  • 2.09 NSFW – Any content not safe for teens. Strictly forbidden.
  • 2.10 Loophole – Attempts to avoid enforcement by exploiting vague language. Staff may treat these as violations.

Section 3 – Law Classes & Penalties

  • Class 1 – Minor Offense → Minimum Penalty: Mute → Repeated → Escalates to Class 2
  • Class 2 – Moderate Offense → Minimum Penalty: Warning → Repeated → Escalates to Class 3
  • Class 3 – Severe Offense → Minimum Penalty: Kick/Ban → Extreme/Recurrent → Permanent Ban

Section 4 – Class 1: Minor Offenses (Mute)

Objective: Correct minor disruptions that interfere with conversation or channels without harming members.

  • 1.01 Spam / Flooding – Posting consecutive messages, emojis, GIFs, or stickers in under 2 minutes.
  • 1.02 Off-Topic Posting – Posting unrelated content repeatedly in a channel.
  • 1.03 Excessive Caps / Emoji Abuse – All caps, excessive emojis, or decorative fonts that reduce readability.
  • 1.04 Minor Spoilers – Revealing story/game/media plot points without spoiler tags.
  • 1.05 Mild Swearing – Casual swearing not directed at anyone.
  • 1.06 Single-Word Spam – Repeated one-word messages.
  • 1.07 Minor Trolling / Jokes – Playful teasing or joking that annoys but does not harass.
  • 1.08 Reaction Flooding – Excessive reactions to messages.
  • 1.09 Minor Role Mention Abuse – Tagging staff or roles unnecessarily once.
  • 1.10 Link Misuse – Posting links outside designated channels once.
  • 1.11 Duplicate Messages – Posting identical messages rapidly.
  • 1.12 Excessive Thread Pings – Tagging the same member repeatedly.
  • 1.13 Minor Self-Promotion – Sharing personal projects once outside approved channels.
  • 1.14 Minor Off-Topic Debate – Discussing slightly off-topic issues without escalating.
  • 1.15 Minor Rule Circumvention Attempt – Testing minor loopholes without malicious intent.
  • 1.16 Background Noise – Minor mic or environmental noise.
  • 1.17 Talking Over Others – Brief interruptions.
  • 1.18 Mic Misuse / Low-Level Trolling – Random sounds mildly disrupting others.
  • 1.19 Voice Ping Misuse – Accidental excessive @everyone/@here pings in voice.
  • 1.20 Minor Roleplay / Joking – Light jokes that do not interrupt discussion.
  • 1.21 Minor Image Flooding – Several images posted rapidly.
  • 1.22 Large File Posting – Slightly oversized files.
  • 1.23 Minor Spoiler Images – Plot images without tags.
  • 1.24 Reaction / GIF Flooding – Excessive GIFs disrupting conversation.
  • 1.25 Minor Meme Off-topic – Memes in wrong channels once.
  • 1.26 Mild Argument Escalation – Slight tension without attacks.
  • 1.27 Mild Teasing – Playful comments annoying but not harassing.
  • 1.28 Incomplete Reporting – Accidental unclear or incomplete reports.
  • 1.29 Ignoring Staff Once – First-time accidental disregard.
  • 1.30 Mislabeling Content – Using incorrect tags or posting slightly off-topic.
  • 1.31 Minor Role Misuse – Tagging roles unnecessarily once.
  • 1.32 Minor Bot Misuse – Triggering bot commands accidentally.
  • 1.33 Minor Channel Misuse – Posting content slightly outside designated channels.
  • 1.34 Temporary Rule Bypass Attempt – Minor loophole testing.
  • 1.35 Minor Voice/Text Integration Abuse – Mild voice + text disruption.

Section 5 – Class 2: Moderate Offenses (Warning)

Objective: Address repeated minor offenses and moderate disruptions.

  • 2.01 Persistent Spam – Continuing spam after Class 1 warnings.
  • 2.02 Repeated Off-Topic Posting – Habitual unrelated content.
  • 2.03 Deliberate Trolling – Intentionally provoking or confusing members.
  • 2.04 Misleading Information – Posting false or deceptive content.
  • 2.05 Excessive Pinging – Tagging repeatedly after warnings.
  • 2.06 Repeated Minor Violations – Multiple Class 1 offenses.
  • 2.07 False / Incomplete Reporting – Misleading or incomplete reports repeatedly.
  • 2.08 Mild Harassment – Repeated teasing or mocking.
  • 2.09 Minor Lying / Manipulation – Pressuring members with false info.
  • 2.10 Channel Disruption – Repeatedly slowing conversation or confusing members.
  • 2.11 Consistent Interrupting – Talking over others repeatedly.
  • 2.12 Voice Channel Trolling – Disruptive sounds meant to annoy.
  • 2.13 Unnecessary Noise – Persistent background noise disturbing conversation.
  • 2.14 Multiple Channel Spamming – Jumping across channels to annoy.
  • 2.15 Repeated Roleplay Abuse – Roleplay disrupting conversation repeatedly.
  • 2.16 Media Flooding – Excessive images, videos, or GIFs.
  • 2.17 Disturbing Content – Non-NSFW content meant to shock.
  • 2.18 Meme Misplacement Repetition – Posting memes in wrong channels repeatedly.
  • 2.19 Reaction Abuse – Habitual excessive reactions.
  • 2.20 Repeated Minor Spoilers – Spoiler images posted repeatedly without tags.
  • 2.21 Repeated Mild Argument Escalation – Continuing minor disagreements.
  • 2.22 Persistent Teasing – Annoying a member repeatedly.
  • 2.23 Ignoring Warnings – Disregarding Class 1 or 2 warnings.
  • 2.24 Disrespect Toward Staff – Repeated minor disrespect.
  • 2.25 Disruption of Group Activities – Interfering with games/events.
  • 2.26 Repeated Minor Role Misuse – Tagging roles repeatedly.
  • 2.27 Repeated Bot Misuse – Misusing bots multiple times.
  • 2.28 Channel Misuse – Posting content repeatedly in wrong channels.
  • 2.29 Minor Attempt to Bypass Rules – Circumventing rules after warnings.
  • 2.30 Minor Permission Abuse – Using roles/permissions to bypass limits.

Section 6 – Class 3: Severe Offenses (Kick / Ban)

Objective: Protect member safety and server integrity. Immediate kick/ban for violations.

  • 3.01 Threats of Violence – Direct or implied threats.
  • 3.02 Doxxing / Sharing Personal Information – Posting private info without consent.
  • 3.03 Persistent Harassment – Targeting members after Class 2 warnings.
  • 3.04 Extreme Trolling / Sabotage – Coordinated disruption or false info.
  • 3.05 Impersonation with Harmful Intent – Pretending to be staff or members to deceive.
  • 3.06 Mass Spamming / Raiding – Posting excessive content across channels.
  • 3.07 Voice Threats – Threatening members verbally.
  • 3.08 Persistent Disruption – Repeatedly disturbing multiple voice channels.
  • 3.09 Evading Penalties via Voice – Using voice to avoid enforcement.
  • 3.10 Coordinated Raids – Planning or participating in disruptive raids.
  • 3.11 Illegal Content Sharing – Posting illegal materials.
  • 3.12 Graphic Disturbing Content – Media intended to shock or harm.
  • 3.13 Malware / Unsafe Links – Malicious files or links.
  • 3.14 Extortion / Blackmail – Attempting to coerce members.
  • 3.15 Severe Manipulation – Deception causing harm.
  • 3.16 Evading Penalties – Using alternate accounts to avoid consequences.
  • 3.17 Repeated Class 2 Violations – Ignoring moderate offense warnings.
  • 3.18 Threats to Server Integrity – Attempting to hack, crash, or sabotage server.
  • 3.19 Hacking / Bot Exploits – Using scripts to damage server function.
  • 3.20 Major Role Abuse – Using roles to bypass rules or manipulate members.
  • 3.21 Server Sabotage – Mass deletion, mass pings, or channel destruction.
  • 3.22 Permission Circumvention – Attempting to override staff permissions.

Section 7 – Enforcement Guidelines

  • Enforcement must cite rule numbers and class.
  • Repeated offenses escalate automatically.
  • Members may appeal respectfully citing the rule.
  • Staff must exercise fairness, judgment, and transparency.
  • Enforcement logs may be kept for accountability.
  • Warnings are given before escalation, except for Class 3 offenses.
